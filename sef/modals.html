There are three species of zebras: the plains zebra, the mountain zebra and the Grvys zebra. The plains zebra and the mountain zebra belong to the subgenus Hippotigris, but Grvys zebra is the sole species of subgenus Dolichohippus. The latter resembles an ass, to which it is closely related, while the former two are more horse-like. All three belong to the genus Equus, along with other living equids.
